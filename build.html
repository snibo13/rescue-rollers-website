Build a RESCUE Roller

The RESCUE roller is a 3D-printed, modular, and customizable roller for the RESCUE project. It is designed to be easy to build and modify, allowing users to create their own unique versions.

This guide provides step-by-step instructions on how to build your own RESCUE Roller, including information on the required parts, assembly instructions, and customization options.

Printed Parts

The RESCUE Roller consists of several 3D-printed parts. You can download the STL files for these parts as of May 30th by clicking this link.

Originally all of these parts were designed for a Bambu Lab P1S printer, but they can be printed on any printer that can handle the size and resolution. The parts are designed to be printed with a 0.4mm nozzle, and the recommended layer height is 0.2mm. The parts can be printed with PLA, PETG, or ABS, but PLA is recommended for ease of printing and post-processing. The friction fit between the wheels and the motor shafts may require some tuning on non Bambu Lab printers, as the tolerances may vary slightly.
